Crafting Basics

The 5 Crafting Systems

Every crafting activity in Diablo 4 runs through one of these five systems. Know your NPC.

🧙
The Occultist
Enchanting & Aspects
  • Enchanting — Reroll one affix on any item
  • Socketing — Add sockets to jewelry
  • Extracting Aspects — Pull legendary powers into your Codex of Power
  • Imprinting — Apply Codex aspects to other gear
Your main crafting NPC. Always extract useful aspects before salvaging!
⚒️
The Blacksmith
Tempering & Upgrading
  • Tempering — Add or upgrade secondary affixes
  • Masterworking — Fully upgrade tempered affixes (+4 to +20)
  • Upgrading — Upgrade item power (Normal → Exceptional → Elite)
  • Repairing — Fix durability on gear
The heart of endgame gear optimization.
💎
The Jeweler
Gems & Sockets
  • Socketing gems — Add gems to jewelry sockets
  • Upgrading gems — Improve gem tiers using Dust
  • Removing gems — Extract gems without destroying them
Gem upgrades cost gold — save this for when you have spare gold in endgame.
⚗️
The Alchemist
Potions & Elixirs
  • Potion upgrades — Increase healing power and capacity
  • Elixir crafting — Temporary stat buffs (XP, Gold, Resources)
Don't overlook elixirs — XP and Gold elixirs stack with incense for fast Paragon farming.
🔥
Masterworking
Endgame Upgrade Path
  • Use at the Blacksmith after tempering
  • Requires Iron (common) + Crystal (rare)
  • Upgrade tiers: +4 → +8 → +12 → +16 → +20
  • Each tier adds 20% to your temper affix values
Only touch this once your gear has the right temper rolls — masterworking is expensive!

Gear Roadmap

Gear Progression Path

Follow this path to efficiently gear your character from campaign to endgame.

1

Campaign Gear — Levels 1–50

Use whatever drops. Don't invest materials or gold — you'll replace everything quickly. Focus on completing the story and unlocking mount.

Priority: Story Progress
2

World Tier I–II — Levels 25–45

Start looking for items with good stats and build-defining legendary aspects. Don't worry about min-maxing yet — your item power is still too low to matter.

Priority: Finding Build Items
3

World Tier III (Nightmare) — Level 45+

Start hunting for Sacred items (purple tag) with the right stats for your build. Item Power 625. This is where your build actually starts to come online.

Priority: Build-Defining Gear
4

World Tier IV (Torment) — Level 60+

Ancestral items only (green tag). Start tempering your best pieces. Extract any useful aspects from new legendaries before salvaging.

Priority: Tempering Begins
5

Endgame — Paragon 100+

Full optimization loop: Temper → Masterwork → Aspect Extraction → Repeat. Keep grinding for better base rolls on your key items. This is where the real min-maxing happens.

Priority: Min-Max Your Build

Item Tiers

Item Quality Levels

Understand what to keep, salvage, and sell at every quality tier.

Type Quality World Tier What to Look For
Normal ⬜ White WT1–4 Sell to vendor or ignore. No useful properties worth keeping.
Magic 🔵 Blue WT1–4 Salvage for materials (Coiling Wards for enchanting). Sell extras.
Rare 🟡 Yellow WT1–4 Check for good stats before salvaging. Extract useful aspects.
Legendary 🟠 Orange WT1–4 Keep if aspect is useful for your build. Always extract aspects before salvaging.
Sacred 🟣 Purple Tag WT3+ Good stats for WT3 content. Decent pre-endgame bases. Check rolls.
Ancestral 🟢 Green Tag WT4+ Best base for endgame builds. Higher stat ranges than Sacred. Always check for great rolls.
Unique 🔴 Named WT4+ Keep for your build if it fits. Trade if you get duplicates (one trade only per Unique).

Endgame Crafting

Tempering Guide

Tempering is your first major power spike in endgame. Here's how it works.

📋 How to Temper

  1. Unlock tempering at the Blacksmith after completing your first Nightmare Dungeon
  2. Select the gear piece you want to temper
  3. Choose a temper manual appropriate for your class (offensive or defensive)
  4. Each manual has multiple possible affixes — the result is random
  5. If you don't like the result, you can retemper (but you keep the item)

🧱 Temper Slots

Each item has a limited number of temper slots. Different item types have different amounts:

Weapons 1 temper slot
Helm, Chest, Legs 2 temper slots
Gloves, Boots, Off-hand 2 temper slots
Amulet 2 temper slots
Rings 1 temper slot

🔑 Requirements

  • Obducite — S12 material. Farm in Nightmare Dungeons and WT4+ activities.
  • Class crafting materials — e.g., Verdant (Druid), Abstruse (Rogue)
  • Gold

💡 Tips

  • Temper your best base items first — Ancestral items with good rolls.
  • You can retemper unlimited times, but each attempt costs Obducite.
  • Save your Obducite for your endgame build pieces, not transitional gear.
  • Some temper affixes are build-defining — know your build's key affixes.

Endgame Upgrade

Masterworking Guide

Masterworking is the final upgrade step — it amplifies your tempered affixes significantly.

⚒️ How Masterworking Works

Masterworking is applied at the Blacksmith after you've tempered your gear. It upgrades all tempered affixes simultaneously in tiers:

+4 First major milestone — adds 20% to each tempered affix
+8 Second tier — another +20% on top of +4
+12 Third tier — significant power boost
+16 Fourth tier — near-max power
+20 Maximum — fully upgraded tempered gear

📦 Materials

铁
Iron
Common
Drops from monsters, salvage
💠
Crystal
Rare
Nightmare Dungeons, WT4+

⚠️ When to Masterwork

Masterworking is expensive. Only apply it to:

  • Ancestral items with great base stat rolls
  • Pieces that are tempered with the right affixes
  • Your endgame build slots — not transitional gear

📈 Value Stacking

Each masterworking tier adds +20% to tempered affix values. At +20, you've effectively quintupled your temper bonus — this is where the big power comes from in endgame.
