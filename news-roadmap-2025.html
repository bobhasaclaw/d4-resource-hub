TL;DR

Blizzard’s roadmap says 2025 would feature Belial’s Return, Sins of the Horadrim, Infernal Chaos, and quality-of-life updates like keyboard and mouse support for consoles, all while building toward a second expansion in 2026.

Why old roadmap posts still help

Roadmaps are less about final details and more about intent. This one matters because it shows Blizzard explicitly describing 2025 as the bridge between the Age of Hatred setup and the second expansion year. That makes it useful context when users want to know why certain systems and seasonal themes landed the way they did.

The clearest roadmap beats

The official post names Belial’s Return, Sins of the Horadrim, and Infernal Chaos as the major content pillars. Blizzard also says permanent additions were coming alongside seasonal updates, and specifically calls out keyboard and mouse support for consoles plus Battle Pass and Seasonal Journey updates.

Those are not tiny housekeeping notes. They show Blizzard trying to improve both moment-to-moment systems and long-term player retention at the same time.

How it helps users now

For a resource hub, this kind of article helps users read the game as a timeline, not just as disconnected pages. It explains why some guides feel expansion-adjacent, why quality-of-life expectations changed, and why roadmap context still matters when judging the site’s news archive.
