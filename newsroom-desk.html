What the desk does

The newsroom desk is responsible for reading official Diablo IV posts, pulling out the parts that actually matter to players, and connecting those updates to the rest of the hub: builds, season info, classes, tools, and videos.

Desk Principle
Official signal first. Player context second. Next-step links always.
Coverage Map
  • Season: launches, events, live-cycle shifts
  • Patch: systems, balance, practical impact
  • Expansion: Warlock, Lord of Hatred, roadmap arc

How stories get chosen

The desk prioritizes updates that change how people play or what they should click next. That means season starts, patch notes, official reveal posts, event windows, and high-signal recap material like PTR or Campfire Chat summaries.

How summaries are written

Each story is written to answer four questions quickly: what changed, why it matters, who should care, and what to click next. That keeps the newsroom useful even when the original Blizzard source is long or vague.

Why the desk exists

A lot of Diablo news coverage either copies the official post or overreaches into speculation. The desk exists to do something more useful: stay grounded in confirmed information, keep the wording clean, and route players straight into the part of the site that helps them act on the update.
