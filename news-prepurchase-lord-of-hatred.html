What the Lord of Hatred pre-purchase actually gives you

Blizzard’s pre-purchase page is more useful than it first looks because it explains who each edition is for, what unlocks immediately, and how Paladin access fits into the expansion rollout.

TL;DR

Blizzard’s official pre-purchase page says every Lord of Hatred edition includes Paladin early access, Vessel of Hatred, 1 extra stash tab, and 2 additional character slots, while higher editions add cosmetics, pets, Platinum, and Battle Pass value.

Why this page matters beyond sales copy

For a resource hub, this is not just a monetization page. It is a player-decision page. Blizzard explicitly says the Age of Hatred Collection is best for players new to Diablo IV, and it spells out exactly what unlocks immediately versus what waits for April 28, 2026.

The most useful confirmed details

The official post says players who pre-purchase any bundle can play Paladin today. It also says Lord of Hatred includes Vessel of Hatred so new players can jump straight into Nahantu and catch up on the story path that leads into Skovos.

Blizzard further confirms expansion features like War Plans, Echoing Hatred, Fishing, a new Talisman, class skill variants, a fresh take on the Horadric Cube, and broad non-expansion updates like a Loot Filter and Skill Tree rework for all players.

Why it matters for your site

This page is valuable because it helps route two different audiences. Existing players care about the endgame and system overhauls. New players care that the Age of Hatred Collection gives them a cleaner entry point into the current Diablo IV package. That is exactly the kind of distinction a professional resource site should surface clearly.
